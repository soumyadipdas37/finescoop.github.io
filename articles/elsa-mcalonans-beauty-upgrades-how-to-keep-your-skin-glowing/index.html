WHAT'S NOW & HOW TO DO IT: GLOW GETTERS

British beauty expert Elsa McAlonan says the end of August does not mean we need to say goodbye to our glowing skin

It may be the last day of August, but that doesn't mean we have to say goodbye to glowing skin.

Whatever the weather, highlighter can take us seamlessly from summer to autumn, adding a lit-from-within radiance and warm glow to faces and bodies.

Creamy formulas, such as the Bali Body Highlighter Stick, are kinder to mature skin.

Easy to apply, it comes in three shades, blends well and dries quickly.

Or try Kevyn Aucoin's Glass Glow Liquid Illuminator (below middle, £26, spacenk.com) — use along the collarbone, on the fronts of legs, or on shoulders or arms for a healthy glow

If you prefer a powder formula, the new RMS Beauty Living Glow Face and Body Powder has been created to flatter every skin tone.

Wondering if you'll suit this season's eyeliner flick trend, or want to play around with your look?

On Collection Cosmetics' relaunched website you can try out make-up styles virtually, including the budget-friendly Fast Stroke Eyeliner.

ALESHA'S HAIR BARGAINS 

Alesha Dixon has found a savvy way to keep her hair in great condition while taking a break from heated styling tools.

The singer, 41, now lets her hair air-dry, adding moisture, softness and shine with the help of products from the TRESemme Keratin Smooth range.

Her favourites include the Keratin Smooth Mask (now £3.05, boots.com), for a deep treatment, and the Keratin Smooth Shine Oil (£3.84), which nourishes the ends of hair.

WHAT CAN I DO ABOUT MY MASK RASH? 

We've heard a lot about 'maskne' recently — break-outs caused by wearing a face covering — but some people suffer instead from irritated, itchy skin under their masks.

'If you notice an itchy, red rash rather than pimples, you may have contact dermatitis,' says cosmetic scientist Lynne Sanders, founder of Cosmetics A La Carte.

'Consider protecting your skin with a barrier cream. In my view, the best option is Vaseline.'

Vaseline Original should be applied before you put on your mask

Then use a gentle cleanser, such as Cosmetics A La Carte Total Lift Off, and moisturiser when you get home.

SHAMELESS INDULGENCE 

Building on the success of Lancome's citrussy floral fragrance Idole, the new, more flamboyant Idole L'Intense takes the key themes of rose, jasmine and chypre notes and turns up the sensuality.

Honeyed Grasse rose absolute combines with the fresh, green fragrance of Isparta rose petal essence to create the floral background. 

Layered with a musky jasmine trio, with light hints of fruit, patchouli, cedar, sandalwood and Madagascar bourbon vanilla, it's a heady, exotic scent that is less feminine than feminist. 

Lancome makes no bones about the fact it is squarely aimed at 'leaders and innovators of tomorrow'.

So if you're 'strong', 'outspoken' and 'empowered', this may well be the perfume for you.
