The daughter of Australian sporting legends Lisa Curry and Grant Kenny has died. 

Jaimi, 33, lost her battle with a long-term illness at Sunshine Coast University Hospital on Monday morning, the devastated family announced. 

'It is with a very heavy heart that Lisa and I confirm that our beautiful daughter Jaimi has lost her battle with a long-term illness and passed away peacefully in hospital this morning in the company of loving family,' a statement on behalf of Mr Kenny read, The Courier Mail reported. 

'Jaimi will forever be remembered as a caring, bright and loving soul who always put others before herself.

'Our hearts are broken and the pain is immense but we must move forward cherishing every wonderful moment we got to share with our treasured first child.

'We thank the incredible team at the Sunshine Coast University Hospital for their tireless commitment to making her better and giving us as the extra time we were able to spend with her.'

Jaimi is survived by her Ironman father, former pro swimmer mother, brother Jett and sister Morgan.

The statement does not reveal the exact nature of Jaimi’s illness.

Curry and Kenny separated in 2009 after 23 years of marriage, before officially divorcing in 2017.
