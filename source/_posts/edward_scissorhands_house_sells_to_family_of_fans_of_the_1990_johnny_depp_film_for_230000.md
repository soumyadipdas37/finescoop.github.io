A Florida home used in the 1990 film Edward Scissorhands has sold for a fraction above asking price.

The house in the Tampa Bay area town of Lutz was purchased for $230,000 last week by 'a family of Edward Scissorhands fans' according to TMZ.

The sum represented a $6000 increase on the initial asking price of $224,000.

One of the buyers grew up in the area, according to TMZ, and had even witnessed the cult classic being filmed in his area as a child.

The house -that was used for the interior and exterior shots for the Boggs family- received 'about a dozen offers' in its first week on the market, according to the gossip site.  

The Tim Burton directed fantasy romance movie starring Johnny Depp and Winona Ryder was filmed in the town of Lutz in the Tampa Bay area of Florida in 1989. 

The 1,432 square home, featuring three bedrooms and two bathrooms, was listed for $224,000, according to the Tampa Bay Times.
