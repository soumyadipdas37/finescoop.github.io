A woman has died in a horror crash between a sports car and truck in Sydney's south in wet conditions.

Emergency services were called to the intersection of Coward and Bourke streets in Mascot following the collision around 12.30pm on Wednesday.

A NSW Ambulance spokesman told Daily Mail Australia a critically injured middle-aged woman was trapped inside the Ford Mustang GT for a short time but died at the scene, despite paramedics' desperate efforts to save her.

Investigations are continuing after a fatal crash between a car and truck in Mascot

Police remain at the crash scene at the intersection of Coward and Bourke streets

The truck driver suffered minor injuries and was taken to Prince of Wales Hospital for mandatory blood and alcohol testing.

Police are investigating the circumstances surrounding the crash and remain at the scene.

Motorists are urged to avoid the area as several roads remain closed.

A middle-aged woman was trapped inside the car and died at the scene

'Take care in the area & allow extra travel time,' a Live Traffic Sydney alert states.

'All westbound lanes are now closed on Coward St between O'Riordan St & Bourke St. Avoid the area or use an alternative route. One eastbound lane is also closed. Traffic in the area is heavy.'

Bus routes 357, 400, 418 and 420 have also been diverted.

A report will be prepared for the Coroner.
