Eurostar has announced a launch date for its £40 London-to-Amsterdam rail service. 

Rail passengers will be able to travel directly from the capital to the Dutch city from the end of October, the rail company said. 

Launch: Rail passengers will be able to travel directly from the capital to the Dutch city from the end of October

The return service starts on October 26 and will enable travellers to travel to the Netherlands' capital in four hours and nine minutes, with tickets costing at least £40 each way. 

Passengers will also be able to go from Rotterdam, located south of Amsterdam, back to St Pancras International in London in three hours 29 minutes. Tickets go on sale from September 1.
