Donny van de Beek has told his Ajax team-mates he is signing for Manchester United, according to reports. 

The 23-year-old midfielder has been strongly linked with a move to the Red Devils this summer, as Ole Gunnar Solskjaer plans to bolster his midfield options ahead of the 2020-21 season.

Tottenham and Barcelona are also believed to be keen on the Ajax star, who scored ten goals in 37 appearances for the Dutch Eredivisie side last season.

Ajax midfielder Donny van de Beek appears to have chosen Manchester United as his next club

The 23-year-old midfielder (left) has reportedly told team-mates he is joining United this year

But according to Spanish news outlet SPORT, van de Beek has already informed his team-mates of his next destination and will fly to Manchester next week to complete the deal.

The report says Ronald Koeman was keen on bringing his fellow countryman to the Camp Nou this summer but the LaLiga club baulked at the £50million asking price for the midfielder.

Van de Beek scored 10 goals last season and is also wanted by Barcelona and Tottenham

While the transfer window closes on October 5, the Dutch giants would like van de Beek's future sorted by September 13, when they begin their league season against Sparta Rotterdam. 

The 23-year-old has been involved in Ajax's pre-season campaign so far and even played in a couple of friendlies this week. 

The midfielder has made 175 appearances in all competitions for his boyhood club, since coming through the youth ranks in 2015.

He was part of the Ajax side who reached the Champions League last four in 2019 and scored in the last-16, quarter-final and semi-final rounds.
