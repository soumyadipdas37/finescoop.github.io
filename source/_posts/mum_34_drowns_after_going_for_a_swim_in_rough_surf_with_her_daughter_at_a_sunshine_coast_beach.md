A mother has drowned in rough surf while swimming with her daughter in Queensland.

The 34-year-old was pulled from the water by surfers at First Bay in Coolum Beach on the Sunshine Coast just after 7 on Monday morning.

The woman's rescuers did CPR on her on the beach before emergency services including paramedics arrived.

Paramedics took over, but unfortunately the woman was pronounced dead at the scene.

A 34-year-old mother has drowned after going for a swim with her daughter at Coolum Beach (pictured) on Queensland's Sunshine Coast

The Courier Mail reported the mother is from Toowoomba and was holidaying in the area with her extended family.

Police Sergeant Peter Blake described the death as 'tragic'.

A Queensland police spokesperson told Daily Mail Australia they were not treating the death as suspicious.
