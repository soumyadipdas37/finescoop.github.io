Apple suffered several hours of widespread outages across their systems on Tuesday night, affecting iCloud, the App Store and Apple TV, among other services.

It was not clear what was causing the disruption, first reported around 8pm EST on Tuesday. 

By around 11pm, the problems appeared to have been fixed. 

Apple's dashboard, showing the status of their services, was alight during the three hour window with red warning signs, indicating outages.

Apple's dashboard on Tuesday was showing outages across the system 

Apple TV, Apple Arcade, Apple Books, Apple TV, iCloud and iTunes were just some of the services affected.

On the site, Apple said: 'Users are experiencing a problem with this service. We are investigating and will update the status as more information becomes available.' 

Apple is not the only company to experience services outages lately.

Many Microsoft services were affected by a multi-hour outage on Monday, and there was a multi-state 911 outage on Monday as well. 

By 11pm EST on Tuesday all of Apple's systems were back to their normal operations

The App Store was among the services which was down on Tuesday night's outage
