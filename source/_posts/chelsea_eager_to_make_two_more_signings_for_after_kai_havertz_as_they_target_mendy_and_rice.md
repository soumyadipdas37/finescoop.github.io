Chelsea manager Frank Lampard is reportedly looking to make two new signings once the club secure a deal for Kai Havertz.

The Blues have been heavily active in the transfer market during this window, but Lampard is still looking to bring in a goalkeeper and a defensive midfielder, as reported by The Telegraph.

Bayer Leverkusen star Havertz has left the Germany national team's training camp so that he can travel to London and undergo a medical ahead of joining Chelsea.

Frank Lampard is eager for Chelsea to make two more signings once they bring in Kai Havertz

Chelsea want Rennes goalkeeper Edouard Mendy, who the French club value at £30million

West Ham midfielder Declan Rice is another target for Chelsea as they look to strengthen 

Lampard wants further arrivals at Stamford Bridge and is targeting Rennes goalkeeper Edouard Mendy, who is valued at £30million by the French side.

In addition, Chelsea want to bring in a defensive midfielder, with West Ham's Declan Rice their primary target in that regard. 

Chelsea are closing in on the signing of Bayer Leverkusen star Kai Havertz for £90million

Chelsea's total spending for this transfer window is set to exceed £200million once they sign Havertz.

Hakim Ziyech and Timo Werner both arrived at the club, while more recently deals have been secured for Ben Chilwell, Thiago Silva and Malang Sarr.
