Ten young men have been charged with murder after a violent brawl between two groups left a teenager dead. 

Girum Mekonnen, 19, died following a violent attack in a Zillmere park, in Brisbane's north, on Sunday. 

Police executed several warrants across Ipswich and South Brisbane on Wednesday, with ten men between 18 and 32 arrested and questioned.

Ten people are set to be charged with murder after a teenager was killed in a brawl between two groups

The group allegedly confronted another group who had gathered in a park around 5.30pm. 

Three vehicles have also been seized since Sunday.

The men have each been charged with one count of murder, 12 counts each of acts intended to cause grievous bodily harm and one count each of affray and disobedience to lawful order.

The men, who reside in Redbank Plains, Goodna and Bray Park, will appear in the Brisbane Magistrates Court tomorrow.

Investigations are continuing.

A red car believed to be connected to the brawl was towed away from the crime scene about 1pm on Monday

Bloodied bandages were left at the scene where a man died on Sunday night
