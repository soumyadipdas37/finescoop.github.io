Bindi Irwin and her husband Chandler Powell announced they were expecting their first child together last month. 

And on Thursday, the expectant mother revealed she experienced a terrifying encounter during her pregnancy. 

Posting to Instagram, the 22-year explained that she got quite a fright after she discovered a bird watching her sleep. 

Keeping an eye on her: On Thursday, Bindi Irwin (pictured) revealed she experienced a terrifying encounter during her pregnancy after discovering a bird watching her sleep

'I was taking one of those lovely pregnancy naps the other day (first trimester, tired all the time, right?) and jumped a mile when I woke up,' she wrote. 

'This guy was watching me out my window, right next to my head! Had to share... ' 

Alongside the post, Bindi shared a photo of the inquisitive black and white bird staring right into her bedroom window. 

Baby on the way: Bindi Irwin and her husband Chandler Powell (both pictured) announced they were expecting their first child together last month

On Sunday, the Wildlife Warrior shared the first sonogram of the baby to Instagram. 

Bindi excitedly posted a photo of herself and her 23-year-old husband, posing with the scans while cuddling a wallaby joey and koala at Australia Zoo. 

'The animal joeys we care for are excited to meet our human joey,' the mother-to-be began her caption. 

'Baby Wildlife Warrior is about about the size of a hummingbird now! We can’t wait to teach our little one about the importance of protecting our planet and the beauty of the wildlife and wild places we love so much,' she continued.

'Baby Wildlife Warrior is about about the size of a hummingbird now!' Pregnant Bindi Irwin and Chandler proudly shared their baby's first sonogram on Instagram this Sunday 

Bindi finished her caption by thanking her fans for their 'kindness and support on this magical journey'. 

Meanwhile, American wakeboarder Chandler posted a similar photo to his Instagram page, telling fans he 'can't wait for his baby to meet everyone in person'.

'Being a dad is already the highlight of my life,' he added.
