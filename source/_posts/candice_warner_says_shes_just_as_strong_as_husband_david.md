Channel Seven's new military-style reality show SAS Australia will see stars attempt to pass the selection process to join the Australian Army's elite Special Air Service.

And in a trailer released on Sunday, former Ironwoman Candice Warner revealed her daughters Ivy Mae, six, Indi Rae, four, and Isla, one, are what inspired her to sign on. 

The 35-year-old said she wanted to remind her young children shared with cricket star husband David Warner, that 'it's not just daddy who is tough and strong'.

Not just a WAG: Candice Warner (pictured standing), 35, said she's 'just as tough and strong' as her cricket star husband David Warner, in a new trailer for Seven's military-style reality show SAS Australia, released on Sunday 

In the trailer, an emotional Candice announced on camera that she's not just a cricket WAG. 

'I'm doing this for my family to show my kids that it's not just daddy who is tough and strong,' she said.  

The mother-of-two is seen gritting her teeth as she embarked on a set of gruelling challenges, led by former British soldier Ant Middleton.

Two new recruits were also introduced in the trailer: former Miss Universe Australia Erin McNaught and Jackson Warne, the son of cricket star Shane Warne. 

'I'm doing this for the boys,' Erin, 38, said, referencing her sons Evander, five, and Ennio, two, shared with English rapper husband Example.

She continued: 'I'm not just the mum that packs their lunches, I'm much more than that, as every mother is.'

Jackson also announced on camera that he's more than just celebrity offspring.

'I'm not just Shane Warne's son, I'm tougher than that. I want him to be really proud of me,' the 20-year-old said.   

SAS Australia is a remake of the hit British TV show SAS: Who Dares Wins.

Alongside Candice, Erin and Jackson, other stars confirmed include convicted drug smuggler Schapelle Corby, publicist Roxy Jacenko and radio star Merrick Watts. 

Filming began earlier this year in Queenstown, New Zealand, however, it resumed in the Snowy Mountains, New South Wales, due to strict international travel restrictions amid the coronavirus pandemic. 

SAS Australia premieres this October on Channel Seven.
